LAYER 01PROVENANCE & SIGNATURES
05 / 13
Positive evidence · cryptographic

Ask the file who signed it
before asking if it’s fake.

C2PA wraps an asset with a signed manifest. SynthID embeds a watermark before the asset ever leaves the model. Both are forms of proactive proof.

▲  CAVEAT. Missing credentials ≠ fake. Adoption is partial, metadata strips in distribution, watermarks degrade.

LAYER 04MODEL-BASED DETECTORS
08 / 13
The honest slide

Detectors aren’t
solved.

Open-source AUC drops sharply on in-the-wild content. Zero-shot generalization to new generators remains a hard, unsolved problem.

×
Generalization. Models trained on yesterday’s generators degrade against today’s.
×
Distribution drift. Compression, screenshotting, and re-sharing erase the very artifacts detectors rely on.
×
Overconfident UX. A single “fake” badge teaches users not to think.

VI. UXTHE ANALYST SURFACE
12 / 13
Most failures are product failures

Don’t ship one badge.
Ship evidence cards.

P·1
Show signature, provenance, metadata, detector, and context as independent cards.
P·2
Each card carries its own confidence and provenance; never collapse to a single red/green.
P·3
Analysts see all five. Users see what can be explained.
P·4
Decisions: allow, friction, review, reject · not true/false.
